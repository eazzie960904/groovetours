Why Korea
Safe Streets & Low Crime Rate
With low crime rates, the streets of Korea are safe, and theft or assault is hardly seen or heard of.
Additionally, in order to guide and assist tourists, "Tourist Police" can be found in major tourist areas.
A Country that Never Sleeps
Many convenience stores, restaurants, malls, and ATMs are open 24/7.
You can shop, dine, drink, and have fun even in the wee hours of the night.
Foreign-friendly Signs
Most public places, including subway stations and vus stops, have English signs.
Major tourist attractions and establishments have multilingual signs and menus.
English, Japanese and Chines are most common.
Excellent & Fast Medical Services
There are many advanced medical facilities staffed with skilled health-care professionals offering top-notch medical services.
Smaller clinics are conveniently linked with general hospitals via an excellent referral system.
Wi-Fi Heaven
Subway stations, coffee shops, mall, public facilities and most tourist areas
offer fast and free Wi-Fi so that you can look up any information you need.
No Cash, No Worries!
To travel and shop in Korea, all you need is a credit card.
Except for smaller family run business, most places accept credit cards.
Convenient Public Transportation
Extensive subway networks, a nationwide railroad system, and express and intercity buses keep the entire country well-connected.
If you purchase a transportation card, you can get free transfers between subways and local city buses.
Fast & Easy Food Delivery
From famous restaurant chains and gourmet eateries to coffee shops and dessert cafes,
you can have almost everything delivered to pretty much anywhere, including Seoul's Hangang riverside parks
